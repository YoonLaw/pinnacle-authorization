Announcement Regarding Lawsuit Against Pinnacle Claims Management, Inc. For Labor Code Violations

We have filed a Private Attorney General Act lawsuit against Pinnacle Claims Management, Inc. (“Pinnacle”) in Orange County Superior Court, Case No. 30-2015-00788841-CU-OE-CJC.

This is not a lawsuit against you; you are not being sued.

The plaintiff in this lawsuit, Savanna Epps, was formerly employed by Pinnacle and has filed a representative action on behalf of certain current and former non-exempt employees of Pinnacle. If you are or were a non-exempt employee of Pinnacle or Western Growers’ Association who worked in California during the period from February 20, 2014 to the present, you may be a member of the group of employees Ms. Epps seeks to represent.

Ms. Epps alleges that Pinnacle failed to provide legally-mandated meal periods and rest breaks, failed to pay all overtime wages and minimum wages, failed to provide accurate wage statements, and failed to pay all wages on termination of employment. For example, Ms. Epps alleges that employees did not receive a second meal period when they worked more than 10 hours in a day, and that Pinnacle failed to pay employees for all hours worked and failed to pay overtime when employees worked more than 40 hours in a week, amongst other violations.

WHY WE NEED YOUR SIGNED AUTHORIZATION

We plan on using time and payroll records for employees at Pinnacle to establish that Pinnacle employees did not receive a second meal period when they worked more than 10 hours in a day and that Pinnacle did not pay employees overtime when they worked more than 40 hours in a week. In addition, we believe that Pinnacle failed to pay its employees for time spent waiting for their computers to boot and starting their computer systems before logging in. Your time and payroll records will help to establish the amount of unpaid wages and missed meal break penalties that Pinnacle owes its current and former employees. Your time and payroll records will also help to establish that Pinnacle violated the law. That is why we need your help.

Please give us authorization to request your records. Your authorization will help us fight for you and your co-workers to obtain the best result in this litigation. A Private Attorney General Lawsuit is not the same as a class action lawsuit. We do not know who will be in or out of the case until the trial. The best way to make sure you can participate in the possible benefits this lawsuit can bring is to sign an authorization. We are not sure what will happen to those people who do not sign authorizations. There has been no formal ruling by the Court on who is in or if anyone is out; we think it is best to be safe than sorry.

So, to help make sure you get paid if we win, you need to send us back your written authorization.

WE NEED PERSONNEL RECORDS TOO

Pinnacle claims that employees could take second meal breaks or signed second meal period waivers. We need personnel records to establish that Pinnacle employees did not take breaks and did not sign waivers. We also believe that Pinnacle retaliated against employees who complained about the company’s pay and meal break practices. We are looking for people who received write-ups for complaining. That’s why we are asking for performance records and documents you signed (write-ups usually require acknowledgement by the employee).
